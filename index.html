Overview

(3 pgs)

Frame: Creative Workplace

  • PHS will be a small public, district HS in Somerville, MA
  • we'll look and feel more like highly functional creative workplace than traditional school (e.g. research lab, artist studios)
  • where YP work on projects of own design that are hard and meaningful to them

How does creative work in the world happen?

  • large contiguous blocks of time
  • real audience/purpose
  • real tools (e.g. macbooks instead of chrome books)

How do we do this work with novices?

  • conditions of real world — e.g. contiguous time—with support…

Supports

  • Advisories with SA, PD, PM
  • Interdisc., immersive progs which provide frames, exposure, media for projs.
  • Emphasis on comp. + storyt. >> powerful ideas in domains that enable divergence + enhance rigor:
  • Explicit + accessible connections with comm. via org partnerships internships, mentorships

Voc approach to trad. academic subj. is particularly well-suited to our context

  • our audience: folks who haven't thrived in trad. acad. setting who'll benefit from small, intimate setting and hands-on approach
  • our context: Somerville is diverse with significant population of folks struggling in t.a.s. and is proximal to many incredible community resources — colleges, artists, research labs, etc.

What we mean by struggling

folks who've struggled with boredom, IEP indicators for dyslexia, ADD/ADHD, self-concepts of "not being an X person." who are in bottom half of cohort for attendance and/or test scores

6 principles of personalized learning

(8 pgs)

1. Personalized Learning Pathways

Frame:

We don't do pathways, we do individualization

    Pathways

    • even though may be personalized, often about about coveragence, how to get different people to cover same things on similar timescale via diff. media
    • E.g. can cover mitosis phases by wksheet, Kahn Acad., making rap.

Individualization

  • not trying to get individuals to do same tasks in same time frame. Projects are individualized b/c based on tastes and interests and are scoped to what's hard + meaningful for indiv.
  • E.g. SoL program >> same big idea >> different projects — simulations, kinetic sculptures, etc.

How staff enable and keep track of divergence:

  • Enable: Staff facilitate programs: provide frame/media/constraints, which YP can bring based on tastes and interests to, to come up with projs.
  • People exposed to similar ideas/tools/media at similar time in programs
  • Keep track: of different things which resonate w/ individuals and what they do/learn via ILP

2. Competency Based Progression

Frame:

We don't do progression, we do projects organically and then retrospective mapping

Different work, same self-mngmt capacities

  • Instead of convergence — progression to same place— we emphasize divergence. Do diff. things at diff. times
  • cultivating same capacities building to the self-mngt, creative/ intell. depth to do 1000 hr proj. of own design (see proj. cycle)

How we can enable + keep track of people's progress given divergence:

  • We're thinking on a different timescale: thinking in 4 yrs instead of splitting CCS and competencies into 180 sep. chunks. enables more organic project process.
  • Retro Mapping: As projects being done, staff working w YP to retrospectively map onto standards via ILP and can see what ideas/tools/skills people are avoiding and address them w yp

3. Flexible Learning Environments

Frame:

In supporting YP in transitioning from stud. to indep. invest. we're helping YP in translate their tastes/interests into actionable projs. and this requires flexibility

Getting Rid of Rigidity

  • Often rigidity of school is biggest impediment to community partnerships.
  • Schools can't work around other org's needs and require them to completely fit into schools' needs — sched. $, space .
  • In addition to designing for flexibility w/r/t projs. we've designed for flexibility to enable collab. with community partners

Flex. in design to allow for divergent proj. and comm. partnerships

  • flexible schedule
  • flexible $ via purchase cards
  • Flexibility w/r/t staff: Relationships first — SA may be managing PD or PM may be managing SA but staff doing different roles, just have area of responsibility
  • Flexible space: different kinds of space, co-location with other orgs, maker space, hub space.
  • We're not just bringing people in, YP are going out: internships, mentorships

4.Engaged Learning

Frame:

Doing real projects requires having a real audience (outside of teachers). And doing real projects means engaging in many different activities at different times, in different contexts, with different people

What it means to have real audience

  • Papert quote: Sand castles and universe
  • gift (e.g. ex. Connie's project)
  • Installation where seen by peers (e.g. prospective work with Debra Weisberg + Nervous System)
  • On the internet
  • project with/for a client/ local org (e.g. HBS, MIT urban planning)

In diff. times in diff. contexts, dep. on proj. having a real audience will mean engaging in many different activites/ types of work:

  • @ PHS
  • out of PHS
  • in small groups
  • independently
  • with a mentor/ another org.,
  • sending professional emails

5. Next Generation Curriculum and Instruction

Frame:

We don't do curriculum and assessments in a typical sense, we do:

  • projects
  • rooted in computation and storytelling
  • which are documented digitally and mapped to CC standards

For us, the performance task is: Does your project work? Can you explain how it works? Making something that works reliably requires:

  • understanding how it works
  • application of understanding

Our version of next gen: Computation and storteylling

B/c of emphasis on projects using computation, "does it work/how does it work?"" will require engagement with powerful computational (logic, mathematical) ideas.

B/c of our emphasis on storytelling in projs and documentation, "Does it work/How does it work?" will engage reflections about coherence, values, aesthetics, etc.

6. SEL + Academic Mindsets

Frame:

Doing projects that are hard and personally meaningful to you requires social, emotional, and meta-cognitive development.

    What projects require:

  • Getting to know self via excavation/reflections on taste/ interests
  • Self-management: What am I working on next and why?
  • Knowing how to get unstuck, which requires developing resilience (e.g. MJ experiences and importance of analogies)
  • Collaborative projs. require interpersonal and relational mngmnt — delegation, explicit comm unication about roles, etc.

Technology

(2 pgs)

Frame

Looking and feeling like a highly functional creative workplace means that technology will "just" be embedded in our work. At PHS, YP won't learn about tech, they'll use tech. At PHS, tech won't be used to make instruction more efficient per se, it will be used to make construction more deep, divergent, and creative.

Tools

  • Given emphasis on computation doing deep personal creative work with computers… crucial that YP have own comps. that they can do projs.
  • Chrome books are cheap and very limited. Can't do real creative work with them b/c they don't have a hard drive and hard to use.
  • Each person at PHS will have own macbook, phone, tablet so that it's easy to write programs, make films, make images, laser cut, edit images, etc.
  • Over course of 4 yrs, cost of comp. = .76 cents/day slice of pizza/pint of milk per day.
  • Compter/phone/tablet over 4 yrs. = $3.76 per day.

Infrastructure

  • Too often in schools, the wifi doesn't work, very hard to get on.
  • Our management of wifi will feel a lot different
  • When go to MIT, very easy as guest to get on wifi
  • B/c of our autonomies around procurement, we can negotiate our own contracts and we'll be contracting with light tower to provide the wifi servies we'll need.

Sustainability of tech

  • our ILP system will be customized to our needs and will be revised based on our needs.
  • We're not betting on new school products buttools people use that use in world
  • Budgeted for tech. Don't have cafeteria, won't have football team. Spending $ on high quality, real tools and staff.

Staff Selection

Frame:

PHS staff's responsbilities won't look like that of traditional teachers in classrooms separated by discipline. Instead staff will be working in teams, advisories, with a cohort of YP for 4 years, running immersive, interdiscplinary programs, helping with individual projects, connecting YP and families with resources, and doing creative work of their own… this means that our structure, hiring, and evaluation process look a bit different from that of traditional academic contexts.

Hiring
  • Stage 1: Applicants — from within and outside district — hear about us, decide to apply, and submit first part of online application which includes basic information, cv, writing responses.
  • Stage 2: Applicants submit a video response to some questions
  • Stage 3: Interviews and references
  • Stage 4: On-site and decision (2 days)go through a few interviews and working together for a bit.

Staff Selection

Evaluation

Staff's goal setting and evaluation process will rhyme with that of YP at PHS. At PHS staff are not transmitters of information but rather mentors, and project-doers themselves. This means that their evaluation process doesn't look that different from what YP go through.

Just as students go through an process with staff and their ILP to articulate their background, aptitudes, interests, strengths, and goals, staff will do so as well in a process involving their Advisory colleagues and the SSA principal.

Revising their ILPS via

  • Peer reviews
  • Surveys
  • Critiques of creative work in their PoS
